Ramcharitmanas

Ramcharitmanas is an epic poem in the Awadhi dialect of Hindi, composed by the 16th-century Indian devotional poet Goswami Tulsidas (c. 1532–1623). Ramacharitmanas literally means "lake of Rama's deeds". Ramcharitmanas is considered one of the greatest works of Hindi literature. The work was acclaimed as the "lively yoga of Indian culture", "the tallest tree in the magic garden of medieval Indian poetry", "the greatest book of all devotional literature" and "the best and most trusted guide to the popular". has gone. However, he wanted the story of Rama to be accessible to the general public and not just to the Sanskrit-speaking elite. To make Rama's story accessible to the common man as a scholar, Tulsidas preferred to write in Awadhi, which at that time was the common language language in large parts of North India.Tradition has it that Tulsidas faced a lot of criticism from Sanskrit scholars of Varanasi for being Bhashya (literally). However, Tulsidas remained steadfast in his resolve to simplify the knowledge contained in the Vedas, Upanishads and Puranas. After this, his work was accepted by all.

Ironically, when he wrote it, Tulsi was instigated by Hindu fundamentalists (then represented by local priests) to "corrupt" an epic written in Sanskrit. Ramacharitmanas's writings also incorporated many cultural traditions, the most important being the Ramlila tradition, the dramatic legislation of the text. Ramcharitmanas is considered by many to be related to the Saguna school of Bhakti movement in Hindi literature.
